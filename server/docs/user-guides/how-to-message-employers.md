# How to Message Employers on NextStep

## Overview
NextStep's messaging feature lets you communicate directly with employers for jobs you've applied to. This guide covers how to send messages, best practices, and tips for professional communication.

## Prerequisites

### Before You Can Message
You must have:
- ✅ Applied to at least one job
- ✅ Completed your profile
- ✅ Verified your email

### Who You Can Message
You can only message employers for:
- Jobs you've applied to
- Companies where you have active applications

## How to Access Messenger

### Step 1: Navigate to Messenger
1. Click "Messenger" in the navigation menu
2. You'll see the messaging interface

### Step 2: Select a Company
1. On the left side, you'll see a list of companies
2. These are companies where you have applications
3. Click on a company name to open the conversation

### Step 3: Start Messaging
1. Type your message in the text box at the bottom
2. Click "Send" or press Enter
3. Your message appears in the conversation

## Messaging Interface

### Left Panel: Contacts
- **Companies:** List of employers you can message
- **Unread indicator:** Shows new messages
- **Last message:** Preview of most recent message

### Right Panel: Conversation
- **Message history:** All messages with selected company
- **Your messages:** Appear on the right (blue)
- **Employer messages:** Appear on the left (gray)
- **Timestamps:** Show when messages were sent

### Message Input
- **Text box:** Type your message here
- **Send button:** Click to send
- **Character limit:** 1000 characters per message

## When to Message Employers

### Good Reasons to Message

**After Applying:**
- Thank them for considering your application
- Express enthusiasm for the role
- Ask about the timeline

**During Interview Process:**
- Confirm interview details
- Ask clarifying questions
- Follow up after interviews

**Status Updates:**
- Ask about application status (after 1 week)
- Respond to their questions
- Provide additional information

**After Offer:**
- Ask questions about the role
- Discuss start date
- Clarify benefits or salary

### When NOT to Message

❌ Before applying to the job
❌ Multiple times per day
❌ To complain about rejection
❌ To ask why you weren't selected
❌ For jobs you haven't applied to

## Message Best Practices

### Professional Tone
✅ "Thank you for considering my application"
❌ "hey whats up"

✅ "I'm very interested in this opportunity"
❌ "this job looks cool"

✅ "Could you provide more details about..."
❌ "tell me more"

### Be Concise
✅ "I'm following up on my application for the Software Engineer position. Could you share the expected timeline?"
❌ "Hi, I applied to your job and I was wondering if you could tell me when you might be making a decision because I'm really interested and I have other offers but I'd prefer to work for you..."

### Be Specific
✅ "I have a question about the React requirements mentioned in the job description"
❌ "I have a question"

### Be Timely
✅ Respond within 24 hours
❌ Wait several days to reply

## Sample Messages

### Initial Follow-Up
```
Hello,

I recently applied for the [Job Title] position and wanted to express my strong interest in this opportunity. 

I believe my experience with [relevant skill] aligns well with your requirements. I'd be happy to discuss how I can contribute to your team.

Thank you for your consideration.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
```

### Asking About Timeline
```
Hello,

I applied for the [Job Title] position on [date] and wanted to follow up on the status of my application.

Could you share the expected timeline for the hiring process?

Thank you,
[Your Name]
```

### Responding to Interview Request
```
Hello,

Thank you for the interview invitation! I'm very excited about this opportunity.

I'm available [list 2-3 time slots]. Please let me know what works best for you.

Looking forward to speaking with you.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
```

### Following Up After Interview
```
Hello,

Thank you for taking the time to interview me yesterday. I enjoyed learning more about the role and your team.

I'm very interested in this position and believe I would be a great fit. Please let me know if you need any additional information.

Thank you,
[Your Name]
```

### Asking Questions About the Role
```
Hello,

I have a few questions about the [Job Title] position:

1. [Specific question about responsibilities]
2. [Question about team structure]
3. [Question about technologies used]

Thank you for your time.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
```

## Response Times

### When to Expect Replies
- **Business hours:** Faster responses (9am-5pm)
- **After hours:** May take until next business day
- **Weekends:** Typically no responses

### How Long to Wait
- **Initial message:** Wait 2-3 business days
- **Follow-up:** Wait 1 week before following up again
- **Urgent matters:** Mention urgency in message

### If No Response
After 1 week with no response:
1. Send a polite follow-up
2. Keep it brief
3. Don't send multiple follow-ups
4. Consider the application may not be moving forward

## Professional Communication Tips

### Do's ✅
- Use proper grammar and spelling
- Be polite and professional
- Keep messages focused
- Respond promptly
- Thank them for their time
- Proofread before sending
- Use proper greetings and closings

### Don'ts ❌
- Use slang or emojis
- Send multiple messages in a row
- Message late at night
- Be pushy or demanding
- Share personal problems
- Complain about other companies
- Send very long messages

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: No Response After Applying
**Wait:** 1 week
**Message:**
```
Hello, I wanted to follow up on my application for [Job Title]. 
I'm very interested in this opportunity. Could you share any 
updates on the hiring process? Thank you.
```

### Scenario 2: Status Changed to "Interviewing"
**Action:** Check messages for interview details
**If no message:**
```
Hello, I noticed my application status changed to "Interviewing." 
I'm very excited about this opportunity. Could you share the next 
steps? Thank you.
```

### Scenario 3: Received Job Offer
**Action:** Respond within 24 hours
**Message:**
```
Thank you for the offer! I'm very excited about this opportunity. 
I have a few questions about [benefits/start date/etc.]. Could we 
schedule a time to discuss? Thank you.
```

### Scenario 4: Application Rejected
**Action:** Send a gracious thank you (optional)
**Message:**
```
Thank you for considering my application. While I'm disappointed, 
I appreciate the opportunity. I'd love to be considered for future 
openings. Best wishes to your team.
```

## Notification Settings

### Receiving Messages
- Check Messenger regularly for new messages
- Look for unread indicators
- Respond promptly to employer messages

### Message Notifications
- New messages appear in Messenger
- Check your email for notifications (if enabled)
- Refresh the page to see new messages

## Privacy & Safety

### What Employers Can See
- Your profile information
- Your application details
- Your messages in the conversation
- Your resume (if uploaded)

### What Employers Cannot See
- Messages with other employers
- Your other applications
- Your personal contact info (unless you share it)

### Safety Tips
- Keep communication on the platform
- Don't share sensitive personal information
- Report inappropriate messages
- Be professional at all times

## Troubleshooting

### Can't See a Company
**Reason:** You haven't applied to their jobs
**Solution:** Apply to a job first, then message

### Messages Not Sending
**Check:**
- Internet connection
- Message isn't too long (1000 char limit)
- You're logged in
- Try refreshing the page

### No Response from Employer
**Possible reasons:**
- They're reviewing many applications
- They're not actively hiring anymore
- They'll respond when they have updates
- Wait 1 week before following up

### Employer Stopped Responding
**What to do:**
- Send one polite follow-up after 1 week
- If still no response, focus on other opportunities
- Don't send multiple follow-ups

## Best Practices Summary
