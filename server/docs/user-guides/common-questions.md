# Common User Questions & Real-Life Scenarios

## Getting Started

### "I just signed up, what do I do first?"
1. Complete your profile (name, email, phone, location)
2. Upload your resume - our AI will extract your skills automatically
3. Review and add any missing skills
4. Start browsing jobs on the homepage
5. Swipe right on jobs you like or click "Apply"

### "I don't have a resume, can I still use NextStep?"
Yes! You can manually add your skills in your profile. However, uploading a resume helps employers learn more about you and increases your chances of getting interviews.

### "How do I know if my profile is complete?"
Your profile is complete when you have:
- First and last name
- Email address
- Phone number
- Preferred location
- At least 5 skills listed
- Resume uploaded (recommended)

### "What if I make a mistake during signup?"
You can update everything in your Profile page except your email. If you need to change your email, contact support.

## Job Search

### "I can't find any jobs in my area"
Try these:
- Search for "remote" jobs - work from anywhere!
- Expand your location search to nearby cities
- Look for hybrid positions
- Consider relocation for the right opportunity

### "All the jobs require experience I don't have"
- Look for "entry level" or "junior" positions
- Focus on transferable skills you do have
- Apply anyway - requirements are often flexible
- Highlight your willingness to learn

### "How do I search for specific types of jobs?"
Use natural language in the search bar:
- "remote software engineer jobs"
- "entry level marketing in Chicago"
- "data analyst paying over 70k"
- "part-time graphic designer"

### "The job I want disappeared"
If you swiped left (passed), it won't show again. If you swiped right (applied), check "My Jobs" to see your application.

### "Can I see jobs I passed on?"
No, once you swipe left, that job won't appear again. Be careful when swiping!

### "How many jobs should I apply to?"
Aim for 10-20 applications per week. Quality matters, but quantity increases your chances. Apply to jobs you're genuinely interested in.

## Applications

### "I applied by accident, what do I do?"
Go to "My Jobs" and click the "Withdraw" button next to that application. This removes your application.

### "Can I apply to the same job twice?"
No, you can only apply once per job. If you withdraw, you can reapply later.

### "How long until I hear back?"
- Most employers respond within 3-7 days
- Some take 2-3 weeks
- If no response after 2 weeks, the position may be filled
- Follow up after 1 week if you're very interested

### "What does each status mean?"
- **Pending** - Employer is reviewing your application
- **Interviewing** - They want to interview you! Check your messages
- **Offered** - You got a job offer! Congratulations!
- **Rejected** - They decided not to move forward

### "I got rejected, what did I do wrong?"
Rejection is normal! It doesn't mean you did anything wrong:
- They may have found someone with more experience
- They may have hired internally
- The position may have been filled
- Your skills may not have been the perfect match
- Keep applying - the right job is out there!

### "Should I follow up on my application?"
Yes! After 1 week, message the employer through the Messenger to express continued interest.

### "Can I withdraw after getting an interview?"
Yes, you can withdraw at any time. Just click "Withdraw" in "My Jobs". Be professional - you might want to work there in the future!

## Profile & Resume

### "How do I update my skills?"
1. Go to Profile page
2. Type a skill in the "Add a new skill" box
3. Click "Add Skill"
4. Click "Save Profile" when done

### "The AI extracted wrong skills from my resume"
No problem! You can:
- Remove incorrect skills by clicking the X
- Add missing skills manually
- Click "Reset Skills" to start over

### "Should I add soft skills or just technical skills?"
Add both! Include:
- Technical skills (Python, Excel, Photoshop)
- Soft skills (Leadership, Communication, Problem Solving)
- Tools (Git, Salesforce, Google Analytics)
- Languages (Spanish, Mandarin)
- Certifications (PMP, AWS Certified)

### "My resume won't upload"
Check that:
- File is PDF, DOC, or DOCX format
- File size is under 10MB
- File isn't password protected
- Try a different browser if issues persist

### "Can employers see my phone number?"
Yes, employers can see your contact information when you apply to their jobs. This is how they'll contact you for interviews!

### "I moved to a new city, how do I update my location?"
Go to Profile, update your "Preferred Location", and click "Save Profile". Your job recommendations will update automatically.

## Messaging

### "An employer messaged me, how do I respond?"
1. Go to Messenger page
2. Click on the company name
3. Type your response
4. Click "Send"
5. Respond within 24 hours for best results!

### "What should I say when an employer messages me?"
Be professional and enthusiastic:
- "Thank you for reaching out! I'm very interested in this opportunity."
- "I'd love to schedule an interview. I'm available [list times]."
- "Thank you for the interview! I enjoyed learning about the role."

### "Can I message an employer before applying?"
No, you can only message employers for jobs you've applied to. Apply first, then message!

### "The employer isn't responding to my messages"
- Wait 2-3 business days
- Send one polite follow-up
- Don't send multiple messages
- They may be busy or interviewing other candidates

### "Can I send my portfolio or additional documents?"
Currently, you can only upload one resume. Mention in your message that you have a portfolio and provide a link (like your website or LinkedIn).

## Interviews

### "I got an interview request, what now?"
1. Respond within 24 hours
2. Confirm your availability
3. Ask about interview format (phone, video, in-person)
4. Research the company
5. Prepare questions to ask them
6. Dress professionally
7. Be on time!

### "How do I prepare for an interview?"
- Research the company and role
- Review the job description
- Prepare examples of your work
- Practice common interview questions
- Prepare questions to ask them
- Test your tech if it's a video interview
- Have your resume handy

### "What if I need to reschedule an interview?"
Message the employer as soon as possible:
- Apologize for the inconvenience
- Explain briefly (if appropriate)
- Suggest 2-3 alternative times
- Be flexible

### "Should I send a thank you message after the interview?"
Yes! Within 24 hours, send a message:
- Thank them for their time
- Mention something specific you discussed
- Reiterate your interest
- Keep it brief and professional

## Job Offers

### "I got a job offer, what do I do?"
1. Congratulations! üéâ
2. Review the offer carefully (salary, benefits, start date)
3. Ask questions if anything is unclear
4. Take 1-2 days to think about it
5. Negotiate if appropriate
6. Accept or decline professionally

### "Can I negotiate the salary?"
Yes! It's normal to negotiate:
- Research typical salaries for the role
- Know your minimum acceptable salary
- Be professional and grateful
- Provide reasons for your request
- Be prepared for them to say no

### "I got multiple offers, how do I choose?"
Consider:
- Salary and benefits
- Company culture and values
- Growth opportunities
- Work-life balance
- Commute or remote options
- Team and manager
- Long-term career goals

### "How do I decline a job offer?"
Be professional and grateful:
- Thank them for the opportunity
- Express appreciation for their time
- Keep it brief
- Don't burn bridges - you may want to work there later!

## Problems & Troubleshooting

### "I forgot my password"
Click "Forgot Password" on the login page to reset it via email.

### "I'm not receiving emails from NextStep"
- Check your spam folder
- Make sure your email is correct in your profile
- Add noreply@nextstep.com to your contacts

### "The website is slow or not loading"
- Refresh the page
- Clear your browser cache
- Try a different browser
- Check your internet connection

### "I can't upload my photo"
- Make sure it's JPG, PNG, or GIF
- File size should be under 5MB
- Try a different photo
- Use a different browser

### "My applications aren't showing up"
- Refresh the page
- Make sure you clicked "Apply" (not just viewed the job)
- Check that you're logged in
- Try logging out and back in

## Account Management

### "How do I delete my account?"
Contact support to request account deletion. We'll remove your data in accordance with privacy regulations.

### "Can I have multiple accounts?"
No, one account per person. If you need to change your email, contact support.

### "How do I change my password?"
Currently, use the "Forgot Password" feature on the login page to reset your password.

### "Is my information secure?"
Yes! We use industry-standard encryption and security practices. Your password is hashed, and your data is protected.

### "Who can see my profile?"
Only employers whose jobs you've applied to can see your full profile. Your information is not publicly visible.

## For Different Situations

### "I'm a recent graduate with no experience"
- Highlight your education and coursework
- Include internships, volunteer work, or projects
- Focus on transferable skills
- Look for entry-level positions
- Emphasize your willingness to learn

### "I'm changing careers"
- Focus on transferable skills
- Explain your motivation in messages
- Look for roles that bridge your old and new career
- Consider entry-level positions in your new field
- Highlight relevant coursework or certifications

### "I'm currently employed and job searching"
- Keep your search confidential
- Update your profile during off-hours
- Be selective about which jobs you apply to
- Respond to messages promptly but discreetly
- Be honest about your start date availability

### "I'm unemployed and need a job quickly"
- Apply to 20+ jobs per week
- Be open to temporary or contract work
- Consider roles slightly below your experience level
- Respond to all messages within hours
- Follow up aggressively
- Network outside of NextStep too

### "I'm looking for remote work only"
- Search for "remote" in the search bar
- Set your location to "Remote"
- Apply to remote-friendly companies
- Mention your remote work experience
- Highlight your self-motivation and communication skills

### "I have a disability and need accommodations"
- You're not required to disclose during application
- Mention accommodations after receiving an offer
- Focus on your skills and qualifications
- Employers must provide reasonable accommodations

### "English is not my first language"
- Use the chatbot to help with messages
- Have someone review your profile
- Be honest about your language skills
- Highlight multilingual abilities as a strength
- Practice interview responses
