# Staying Motivated During Your Job Search

## Common Feelings & How to Handle Them

### "I'm feeling discouraged"
**This is completely normal!** Job searching is hard. Here's what helps:
- Take a break for a day
- Celebrate small wins (completed profile, submitted application)
- Remember: every "no" gets you closer to "yes"
- Talk to friends or family
- Set smaller, achievable goals
- Focus on what you can control

### "I keep getting rejected"
**Rejection is part of the process!** Even successful people face rejection:
- Most people apply to 20-50 jobs before getting hired
- Rejection doesn't mean you're not good enough
- Each rejection is practice for the next opportunity
- Learn from each experience
- Keep improving your profile and approach
- The right job is still out there!

### "I'm not hearing back from anyone"
**This happens to everyone!** Try these:
- Apply to more jobs (aim for 15-20 per week)
- Improve your profile and resume
- Broaden your search criteria
- Follow up on applications after 1 week
- Ask for feedback when possible
- Be patient - hiring takes time

### "I'm overwhelmed and don't know where to start"
**Break it down into small steps:**
1. Today: Complete your profile
2. Tomorrow: Upload your resume
3. Day 3: Apply to 3 jobs
4. Day 4: Apply to 3 more jobs
5. Day 5: Follow up on applications

One step at a time! You don't have to do everything at once.

### "I'm worried I'm not qualified enough"
**Imposter syndrome is real!** Remember:
- Job requirements are wish lists, not requirements
- You have more skills than you think
- Companies can train you on specifics
- Your unique perspective is valuable
- Apply anyway - let them decide!

### "I've been searching for months with no luck"
**Long searches are tough but common:**
- Review and update your strategy
- Get feedback on your resume
- Expand your search criteria
- Consider temporary or contract work
- Network outside of NextStep
- Take care of your mental health
- Don't give up - timing matters!

## Motivation Strategies

### Set Daily Goals
Instead of "get a job" (too big), try:
- Apply to 3 jobs today
- Update my profile
- Research 5 companies
- Practice interview questions for 30 minutes

### Track Your Progress
Keep a simple log:
- Week 1: 10 applications, 0 responses
- Week 2: 15 applications, 2 responses
- Week 3: 12 applications, 1 interview!
- Seeing progress helps motivation!

### Celebrate Small Wins
Every step forward counts:

- ‚úÖ Resume uploaded
- ‚úÖ First application submitted
- ‚úÖ 10 applications submitted

- ‚úÖ Job offer received!

### Create a Routine
Make job searching a habit:
- Morning: Apply to 3 jobs
- Afternoon: Research companies, update profile
- Evening: Review applications
- Consistency beats intensity!

### Take Breaks
Job searching is exhausting:
- Take weekends off
- Do activities you enjoy
- Exercise and eat well
- Sleep enough
- Talk to supportive people
- Remember: you're more than your job search!

## When You Need Extra Support

### Talk to Someone
- Friends and family
- Career counselors
- Support groups
- Online communities
- Mentors in your field

### Use the Chatbot
Ask for help:
- "I'm feeling discouraged, what should I do?"
- "How can I improve my chances?"
- "What am I doing wrong?"
- "Give me motivation"

### Remember Your Why
Why do you want a new job?
- Better salary?
- Career growth?
- Work-life balance?
- New challenges?
- Closer to home?

Keep your "why" in mind when motivation is low.
