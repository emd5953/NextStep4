# How to Post Jobs on NextStep (Employer Guide)

## Overview
As an employer on NextStep, you can post job openings to reach qualified candidates. Our AI-powered matching system connects your jobs with the right talent.

## Prerequisites

### Before Posting Jobs
- ✅ Employer account created
- ✅ Company profile completed
- ✅ Verified email address
- ✅ Employer permissions enabled

## Step-by-Step: Post a New Job

### Step 1: Access Job Posting
1. Log in to your employer account
2. Go to "Employer Dashboard"
3. Click "Post New Job" or "Manage Job Postings"
4. Click the "Create New Job" button

### Step 2: Enter Job Details

**Job Title** (Required)
- Be specific and clear
- Use standard job titles
- Examples: "Senior Software Engineer", "Marketing Manager", "Data Analyst"
- Avoid: "Rockstar Developer", "Ninja Coder"

**Company Name** (Auto-filled)
- Your company name from profile
- Appears on all job listings
- Candidates see this when browsing

**Company Website** (Optional)
- Your company's website URL
- Helps candidates research your company
- Example: https://www.yourcompany.com

### Step 3: Job Description

**What to Include:**
- Overview of the role
- Key responsibilities (3-5 bullet points)
- Day-to-day tasks
- Team structure
- Growth opportunities

**Example:**
```
We're seeking a Senior Software Engineer to join our growing team. 
You'll work on building scalable web applications using modern 
technologies.

Responsibilities:
• Design and develop new features
• Collaborate with product and design teams
• Mentor junior developers
• Participate in code reviews
• Contribute to technical decisions
```

**Best Practices:**
- Keep it concise (200-400 words)
- Use bullet points
- Focus on what they'll do
- Highlight unique aspects
- Be honest about challenges

### Step 4: Required Skills

**How to Add Skills:**
1. Type a skill in the skills field
2. Press Enter or click Add
3. Repeat for all required skills
4. You can add multiple skills

**Skill Guidelines:**
- List 5-10 key skills
- Be specific: "React.js" not just "JavaScript"
- Include must-have skills only
- Separate nice-to-have skills in description
- Use industry-standard terms

**Examples by Role:**

**Software Engineer:**
- JavaScript, React, Node.js, PostgreSQL, Git, AWS

**Marketing Manager:**
- SEO, Google Analytics, Content Strategy, Social Media, Email Marketing

**Data Analyst:**
- SQL, Python, Tableau, Excel, Statistics

### Step 5: Location

**Location Options:**
- Specific city: "Chicago, IL"
- Multiple locations: "New York, NY; San Francisco, CA"
- Remote: "Remote"
- Hybrid: "Chicago, IL (Hybrid)"

**Tips:**
- Be clear about remote options
- Specify if relocation is required
- Mention if multiple offices available

### Step 6: Salary Range (Optional but Recommended)

**Why Include Salary:**
- Attracts more qualified candidates
- Reduces time wasted on mismatched expectations
- Shows transparency
- Required in some states

**Format:**
- "$80,000 - $100,000"
- "$50k-$70k"
- "Competitive"
- "DOE" (Depends on Experience)

**Best Practice:**
- Include actual range when possible
- Be realistic and competitive
- Consider total compensation

### Step 7: Benefits (Optional)

**Common Benefits to List:**
- Health insurance
- Dental and vision
- 401(k) matching
- Paid time off
- Remote work options
- Flexible hours
- Professional development
- Stock options
- Gym membership
- Commuter benefits

**How to Add:**
- Type each benefit
- Press Enter to add
- Add as many as applicable

### Step 8: Work Schedule (Optional)

**Examples:**
- "Full-time, Monday-Friday, 9am-5pm"
- "Full-time, flexible hours"
- "Part-time, 20 hours/week"
- "Contract, 3-month project"

### Step 9: Review and Post

1. **Review all information**
   - Check for typos
   - Verify salary and benefits
   - Ensure skills are correct
   - Read description aloud

2. **Click "Post Job"**
   - Job is immediately live
   - Appears in candidate searches
   - Added to your job listings

3. **Confirmation**
   - You'll see a success message
   - Job appears in "Manage Job Postings"
   - Candidates can now apply

## After Posting

### What Happens Next

**Immediate:**
- Job goes live on NextStep
- Appears in search results
- Matched with relevant candidates
- Shows on your company profile

**Within 24 Hours:**
- AI matches job with candidate profiles
- Job appears in personalized feeds
- Candidates can apply

**Ongoing:**
- Receive applications
- Track in Application Tracker
- Message candidates
- Update job status

### Managing Your Job Posting

**View Applications:**
1. Go to "Application Tracker"
2. See all applicants for this job
3. Review resumes and profiles
4. Update application statuses

**Edit Job Posting:**
1. Go to "Manage Job Postings"
2. Find your job
3. Click "Edit"
4. Make changes
5. Click "Save"

**Delete Job Posting:**
1. Go to "Manage Job Postings"
2. Find your job
3. Click "Delete"
4. Confirm deletion
5. Job is removed (cannot be undone)

## Job Posting Best Practices

### Write Clear Descriptions
✅ "Build and maintain React applications"
❌ "Do cool stuff with code"

### Be Specific About Requirements
✅ "3+ years of Python experience"
❌ "Some programming experience"

### Highlight What Makes You Special
✅ "Work on products used by 1M+ users"
❌ "We're a great company"

### Be Honest About Challenges
✅ "Fast-paced startup environment"
❌ "Perfect work-life balance" (if not true)

### Use Inclusive Language
✅ "We welcome candidates from all backgrounds"
❌ "Looking for young, energetic rockstars"

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Vague Job Titles
**Problem:** "Tech Person Needed"
**Solution:** "Full Stack Software Engineer"

### ❌ Too Many Requirements
**Problem:** Listing 20+ required skills
**Solution:** Focus on 5-7 must-have skills

### ❌ No Salary Information
**Problem:** "Competitive salary"
**Solution:** "$80,000 - $100,000"

### ❌ Copy-Paste Descriptions
**Problem:** Generic job description
**Solution:** Customize for your company

### ❌ Unrealistic Expectations
**Problem:** "Entry level, 5 years experience required"
**Solution:** Be realistic about experience levels

## Optimizing Your Job Posting

### For Better Matches
- Complete all fields
- Use specific skills
- Include salary range
- Add comprehensive benefits
- Write detailed description

### For More Applications
- Competitive salary
- Clear growth opportunities
- Highlight company culture
- Mention unique perks
- Be transparent about role

### For Quality Candidates
- Specific requirements
- Realistic expectations
- Clear responsibilities
- Honest about challenges
- Professional tone

## Job Posting Checklist

Before posting, verify:
- [ ] Clear, specific job title
- [ ] Detailed job description (200-400 words)
- [ ] 5-10 required skills listed
- [ ] Location specified (or "Remote")
- [ ] Salary range included (recommended)
- [ ] Benefits listed
- [ ] Work schedule mentioned
- [ ] No typos or errors
- [ ] Inclusive language used
- [ ] Realistic requirements

## Tracking Performance

### Monitor Your Job
- Number of views
- Number of applications
- Application quality
- Time to fill

### Adjust If Needed
- Not enough applications? Broaden requirements
- Too many applications? Be more specific
- Wrong candidates? Clarify skills needed
- No applications? Check salary competitiveness
