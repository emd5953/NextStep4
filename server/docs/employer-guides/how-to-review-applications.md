# How to Review Applications (Employer Guide)

## Overview
The Application Tracker helps you manage all applications for your job postings. Review candidates, update statuses, and communicate with applicants all in one place.

## Accessing Application Tracker

### Step 1: Navigate to Tracker
1. Log in to your employer account
2. Click "Application Tracker" in navigation
3. You'll see all applications for your jobs

### What You'll See
- List of all applicants
- Job titles they applied for
- Application dates
- Current status
- Applicant information
- Action buttons

## Understanding the Application Tracker

### Table Columns

**Applicant Name**
- Full name of the candidate
- Click to view full profile

**Position**
- Job title they applied for
- Links to job posting

**Date Applied**
- When they submitted application
- Sorted by most recent first

**Status**
- Current application status
- Color-coded for quick scanning

**Notes**
- Your internal notes
- Not visible to candidates

**Actions**
- Edit status
- View profile
- Message candidate

## Application Statuses

### Pending (Blue)
**Meaning:** Application received, under review
**When to use:** Initial status for all new applications
**Next steps:** Review resume and profile

### Interviewing (Orange)
**Meaning:** Candidate is in interview process
**When to use:** After deciding to interview
**Next steps:** Schedule interview, send details

### Offered (Green)
**Meaning:** Job offer extended
**When to use:** After deciding to hire
**Next steps:** Discuss terms, await response

### Rejected (Red)
**Meaning:** Not moving forward
**When to use:** When candidate isn't a fit
**Next steps:** Send rejection email (automatic)

## Step-by-Step: Review Process

### Step 1: Review New Applications

1. **Check Application Tracker Daily**
   - New applications appear automatically
   - Status starts as "Pending"

2. **Click on Applicant Name**
   - Opens full profile
   - View resume, skills, contact info
   - Review work experience

3. **Evaluate Candidate**
   - Do skills match requirements?
   - Is experience level appropriate?
   - Does location work?
   - Is salary expectation aligned?

### Step 2: Update Application Status

1. **Click "Edit" Button**
   - Opens status editor
   - Shows current status

2. **Select New Status**
   - Choose from dropdown:
     - Pending
     - Interviewing
     - Offered
     - Rejected

3. **Add Notes (Optional)**
   - Internal notes for your team
   - Not visible to candidate
   - Example: "Strong React skills, schedule phone screen"

4. **Click "Save"**
   - Status updates immediately
   - Candidate receives email notification
   - Changes are logged

### Step 3: Communicate with Candidates

**Send Message:**
1. Click "Message" button
2. Opens messenger with candidate
3. Type your message
4. Click "Send"

**When to Message:**
- To schedule interviews
- To ask clarifying questions
- To request additional information
- To discuss offer details

## Email Notifications

### Automatic Emails Sent

**When Status Changes to "Interviewing":**
```
Subject: Interview Invitation - [Job Title]

Dear [Candidate Name],

We'd like to schedule an interview for the [Job Title] position.
Please contact us to arrange a suitable time.

Best regards,
[Company Name]
```

**When Status Changes to "Offered":**
```
Subject: Job Offer - [Job Title]

Dear [Candidate Name],

Congratulations! We're pleased to offer you the [Job Title] position.
Please review the offer details and respond by [date].

Best regards,
[Company Name]
```

**When Status Changes to "Rejected":**
```
Subject: Application Update - [Job Title]

Dear [Candidate Name],

Thank you for your interest in the [Job Title] position. After
careful consideration, we've decided to move forward with other
candidates. We wish you the best in your job search.

Best regards,
[Company Name]
```

## Best Practices

### Response Time
- Review applications within 24-48 hours
- Update statuses promptly
- Respond to candidate messages quickly
- Keep candidates informed

### Communication
- Be professional and courteous
- Provide clear next steps
- Set expectations for timeline
- Be honest but kind

### Organization
- Use notes to track candidate details
- Update statuses consistently
- Keep team informed
- Document interview feedback

### Fairness
- Review all applications thoroughly
- Give each candidate fair consideration
- Use consistent evaluation criteria
- Avoid bias in decision-making

## Reviewing Resumes

### What to Look For

**Skills Match**
- Do they have required skills?
- What's their proficiency level?
- Any bonus skills?

**Experience Level**
- Years of relevant experience
- Similar roles or industries
- Career progression

**Education**
- Relevant degrees or certifications
- Continuous learning
- Specialized training

**Work History**
- Job stability
- Career growth
- Relevant companies

### Red Flags
- Frequent job hopping (without explanation)
- Unexplained employment gaps
- Exaggerated claims
- Poor communication in application

### Green Flags
- Clear career progression
- Relevant experience
- Strong skill match
- Professional presentation
- Enthusiasm for role

## Interview Process

### Moving to "Interviewing" Status

1. **Update Status**
   - Change from "Pending" to "Interviewing"
   - Candidate receives email notification

2. **Message Candidate**
   - Propose interview times
   - Share interview format (phone, video, in-person)
   - Provide any preparation materials
   - Include meeting link if virtual

3. **Prepare for Interview**
   - Review resume again
   - Prepare questions
   - Coordinate with team
   - Schedule interview

### After Interview

**If Moving Forward:**
- Update status to "Offered"
- Discuss offer details via message
- Send formal offer letter

**If Not Moving Forward:**
- Update status to "Rejected"
- Automatic email sent
- Optional: Send personalized message

## Making Offers

### Before Extending Offer

**Verify:**
- Candidate is still interested
- Salary expectations align
- Start date works
- References checked (if applicable)

### Extending the Offer

1. **Update Status to "Offered"**
   - Automatic email sent
   - Status changes to green

2. **Message with Details**
   - Salary and benefits
   - Start date
   - Next steps
   - Response deadline

3. **Follow Up**
   - Answer questions
   - Provide additional information
   - Negotiate if needed

### If Offer Accepted
- Coordinate start date
- Send onboarding information
- Update internal systems
- Close job posting (if filled)

### If Offer Declined
- Update status back to "Rejected"
- Move to next candidate
- Keep job posting open

## Rejecting Candidates

### When to Reject
- Skills don't match requirements
- Experience level doesn't fit
- Better candidates available
- Position filled
- Budget constraints

### How to Reject Professionally

1. **Update Status to "Rejected"**
   - Automatic email sent
   - Be timely (don't leave them waiting)

2. **Optional: Personalized Message**
   - Thank them for applying
   - Wish them well
   - Keep door open for future opportunities

3. **Be Kind**
   - Remember they're people
   - Rejection is hard
   - Be respectful and professional

## Managing Multiple Applications

### Prioritization
- Review most recent first
- Focus on best matches
- Set aside time daily
- Don't let applications sit

### Batch Processing
- Review similar applications together
- Update multiple statuses at once
- Send messages in batches
- Schedule interviews efficiently

### Team Collaboration
- Share notes with hiring team
- Coordinate interview schedules
- Discuss candidates together
- Make decisions collectively

## Troubleshooting

### Can't See Applications
**Check:**
- You have active job postings
- Applications exist for your jobs
- You're logged in as employer
- Refresh the page

### Status Won't Update
**Try:**
- Click "Save" after changing
- Refresh the page
- Check internet connection
- Try different browser

### Candidate Not Responding
**Actions:**
- Send follow-up message
- Wait 3-5 business days
- Move to other candidates
- Update status if no response
